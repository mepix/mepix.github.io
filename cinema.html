Cinematic Reel

"That Engineer With a Camera"

  • Linkedin

Lights, Camera, Action!

I started making stop action lego movies in the 5th grade, and have continued to work in the industry. From production assistant to sound engineer to camera to director, I have worked on both student and professional shoots. I spent the summer between high school and college as an assistant editor for Carriage House Media and joined UCLA's Film and Photograph Society as a Freshman. Though I am no longer as active in the industry as I would like to be, I still work on shoots from time to time.

Here is a short sample of some of my work!

Slip Chain

Awards: UCLA Campus Best Picture (2012), Top 25 Films in CMF Hollywood (2012)

Position: First AC

At UCLA, I joined the Film and Photography Society. For the campus movie festival, we had 1 week to shoot and edit a film. I had the pleasure to work with wonderful Ted Sim and Minh Bui for this production.

Flintridge Prep's Idea Lab

Position: Editor, Cinematographer

At Carriage House Media, this was my primary project. I enjoyed editing together the graphics and this entire project.

Echoing Hope Ranch

Position: Editor

While working for Carriage House Media, I had privilege of editing this promotional video for Echoing Hope Ranch. This is one of those pro bono works that just seems like the right thing to do.

Colors Of

Password: beyondtherainbow

Awards: Best Cinematography (2011)

Position: Director, Writer

This film was an early experiment with colors, saturation, and lenses. (The writing is a tad melodramatic.)

Rust In Peace

Password: RedCar

Awards: History Day Los Angeles Top Documentary (2008), State Finalist (2008)

Position: Producer, Director, Editor

This is the documentary showcases the rise and fall of Los Angeles streetcar system in the 20th Century. The Pacific Electric system had over 1000 miles of track and served the entire region, but by the 1970's the system was destroyed. Now, in the 21st Century, we are spending billions of dollars to rebuild a light rail network that is only a fraction of the system's former transit glory.
