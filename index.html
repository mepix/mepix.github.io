Welcome

Welcome

I began my journey playing with Lego robotics and making stop motion films in grade school. I followed my interests, and continued working with cameras and robotics throughout high school. I decided to study mechanical engineering at UCLA after I discovered the magic of 3D Printing. Now, I love the work I do with 3D printing, robotics, and computer vision.

Mechatronics

I live at the intersection of hardware, electronics, and software. I'm comfortable with mechanical and electrical fabrication and assembly as well as C/C++, Python, MATLAB, and LabVIEW.

Engineering + Design

Projects should be designed and manufactured with the end user in mind. I incorporate a basic Design Thinking approach in my projects to ensure the results function and meet the needs of the end user.

Digital Media

My friends called me "that engineer with a camera" because I've always had a passion for movie making. I moonlight on film sets whenever someone needs a cinematographer, sound engineer, or just an extra hand.

Strawberry Transporter

Tanner Research TR1: PUP-E (Picker Utility Productivity Enhancer)

The USDA gave Tanner Research a contract to create a lightweight produce transport robot to work alongside pickers in a field. The goal of these picking aids is to improve the safety and efficiency of the existing labor pool while working towards broader automation. I serve as the primary engineer reporting to the project PI, Dr. John Tanner. I touch most aspects of the robot from design to fabrication and maintain 10,000+ lines of our custom C/C++ code.

  • Date: August 2018 (USDA Grant End)
  • Client: Tanner Research/USDA
  • Category: Professional

The Albatross

Large Format 3D Printer

For my third 3D Printer build, I wanted to go big. I constructed this printer with a 2'x2'x2' build volume for Tanner Research over two consecutive summers. It has a borosilicate glass build platform on top of an AC heated bed. I added a relay and a thermal fuse to safely control the bed temperature the Marlin's PID on the Azteeg board. The frame is mostly aluminum extrusions, with a few waterjetted steel parts.

  • Date: September 2014
  • Client: Tanner Research
  • Category: Professional

Raspberry Pi Tablet

Embedded Computer

My Uncle gave all the cousins in my generation a Raspberry Pi for Christmas, and I decided to turn mine into a tablet! All the woodworking was done by hand (no CNC!) with saws, routers, and chisels.
