Salutations!

Welcome to the website of "that engineer with a camera." My name is Merrick Campbell.

I earned that title when my friends teased me for carrying my DSLR camera around all the time. At first, I laughed it off, but I eventually embraced it because it captures who I am. But, really, only part of who I am: I am an Eagle Scout. I am an engineer. I am a cinematographer. I am an entrepreneur.

Scouting

I joined Troop 4 because I wanted to camp and experience the outdoors. Along the way, I served as Patrol Leader, Troop Guide, and Senior Patrol Leader. I had the honor to be inducted into the Order of the Arrow, the Boy Scout of America's National Honor and Service Society. When I aged out of Troop 4 at the age of 18, I received the Valentine Award for my service to the troop.

My experience with Scouting was so positive, I continue to give back to the Scouting community through Bruin Scouts at UCLA. We mentor current Boy and Girl Scouts throughout the Los Angeles Area and assist them with their service projects. As treasurer, I secured $2000 in student government funding to host over 100 Scouts for our annual College Day.

Engineering Endeavors

I like to think globally, and act locally. Even though I may not revolutionize the world today, my actions can contribute to something larger.

3D Printing drew me towards engineering. At UCLA, I cofounded the intercollegiate network of 3D Printer clubs called 3D4E (3D '4' Everyone)

At Tanner Research, I joined their robotics project to work on an autonomous agricultural robot. The robot is designed to follow pickers in the field and return berries to a designated position. This robot can improve both the lives of the workers and reduce the cost of produce, allowing more people to have healthier lifestyles.

I work at the UCLA Smart Grid Energy Research Center to manufacture electric vehicle chargers because I believe EVs are part of the transportation future.

Cinema & Digital Media

In fifth grade, I directed my first stop action lego movie and shortly thereafter joined my elementary school's MovieMaker Club. I caught the film bug and I spent the next few years of my life producing and directing films. In high school, I joined the Film Club and worked to co-found my high school's film and music festival.

At UCLA, I joined the Film and Photography Society and continued to make films. I have crewed many student films and a few professional shoots, including a Mazda Commercial where we used a really cool camera car!

Entrepreneurial Enterprises

I pursue interesting ideas that seem like the right thing to do. Yes, I sometimes often fail. Yes, I hear the same old "nobody cares" mantra. But I do it anyway.

In 2014, my friend and I started Biznector. Biznector revolutionized logistics by connecting growing businesses with trustworthy logistics providers and fulfillment services. We incorporated, developed a website, and found investors. We were offered a valuation of $500,000 and entrance into an accelerator. Unfortunately, our success did not last, but I learned a lot.

Concluding Comments

The problem with the world is that there are so many interesting ideas out there and not enough time to properly engage with each one.

In the juggling act of life, it is very important to me to spend my time on tasks that matter: pursuing my work passionately and enjoying good company.

I view the world through many lenses to keep things fresh and I hope that somewhere along the way I'll get the chance to do something good.
